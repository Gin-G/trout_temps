Above 65°F, trout can't get the oxygen they need to recover from a fight. This pulls current water temperatures from USGS gages so you can pick a cold stretch — or call it a day.

Reading the gages…
Data: USGS Instantaneous Values service · parameter 00010 (water temperature). Readings are point-in-time at the gage and may differ from where you stand on the river. The 65°F guidance is a widely used rule of thumb for coldwater trout; check local regulations and any voluntary fishing closures.
